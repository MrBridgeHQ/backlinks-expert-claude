# Opportunity Types - A Taxonomy of Link Sources

This page classifies the *kinds of place* a backlink can come from. Where `tactics-tiers.md` answers "what do I *do*", this page answers "what is this *source*, and what is it actually worth". It exists largely to correct the false claims that saturate the open-web link lists - especially inflated dofollow status and recycled authority numbers.

The directing insight: **a link source's value is decided by its dofollow status, its topical relevance, and its real traffic - in that combination, never by a third-party authority score alone.** A high-DR irrelevant nofollow source is worth almost nothing for ranking and little for traffic. A modest, relevant, well-indexed source with real readers can be worth a lot even when nofollow. Read every row below through that lens.

A reminder before the table: **DA (Moz) and DR (Ahrefs) are third-party metrics, not Google signals.** Any value rating here is for triage, not truth. And **dofollow status must be re-verified per page** (`qualification.md`) - the "typical dofollow status" column records the honest *default* for the platform type, which is the opposite of what most listicles claim.

## The taxonomy

### Forums

- **What it is:** Topic-based discussion communities (dev forums, SEO forums, hobby/niche forums). Links appear in posts, profiles, or signatures.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Nofollow / UGC by default.** Most forums moved to `rel="ugc"`/`nofollow` years ago precisely to stop being link farms - the widespread "dofollow forum list" claim is mostly false in 2026 and must be re-checked per page and per placement (post vs profile vs signature can differ).
- **Typical value:** Traffic and brand presence on active, relevant, well-indexed forums; effectively zero ranking credit. A nofollow link on a busy indexed thread still sends qualified visitors.
- **Risk:** Low if you participate genuinely; medium-high if you drop links or lean on signatures (named link spam, and the host may ban you and delete your links).
- **Language availability:** EN - abundant (dev/SEO/hosting/hobby communities). FR - fewer but real (the large FR SEO community, FR business/tech forums). Always confirm the forum is *active* - several frequently-listed forums are dead or unmaintained.

### Profiles / community accounts

- **What it is:** A link in your account profile on a platform (GitHub, dev communities, industry/business directories-with-profiles).
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Mostly nofollow.** A few platforms render profile links as followed; do not assume it.
- **Typical value:** Brand presence and discovery where you genuinely participate (GitHub is the standout for a dev/tool audience - a profile/README link reaches the right people). Minimal-to-zero ranking value.
- **Risk:** Low for a handful of relevant profiles; high if mass-created across unrelated "profile-creation sites" (scaled-abuse spam - the "9,000 sites" trap).
- **Language availability:** EN - abundant. FR - fewer dedicated profile platforms; the global dev platforms (GitHub) serve both.

### Directories

- **What it is:** Curated or open listings of sites/businesses/tools, sometimes by niche or locale.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Mixed.** Reputable niche/local directories may be followed; mass general directories are usually nofollow or simply ignored by Google.
- **Typical value:** Reputable, relevant, traffic-sending directories give discovery and minor value; low-quality directories are named link spam and give nothing. Tool/MCP catalogs are a relevant sub-type (a specific marketplace/directory channel has its own playbook - delegate to a directory/marketplace-distribution skill).
- **Risk:** Low for niche/reputable; high for mass general-directory submission.
- **Language availability:** EN - abundant. FR - solid local/business directories exist.

### Guest posts

- **What it is:** An article you write for another publication, linking back to your property in the body or author bio.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Often dofollow** when editorial - but optimized-anchor guest-post links placed at scale are named link spam, so the *anchor* must stay natural/branded.
- **Typical value:** High - a genuine editorial link from a relevant publication moves rankings and sends traffic. The decisive condition: the post must serve the host's readers, not merely rent the host's domain rating (site-reputation abuse otherwise).
- **Risk:** Low when editorial and naturally anchored; high when it's a paid optimized-anchor placement on a thin host.
- **Language availability:** EN - abundant. FR - abundant (active FR guest-blogging culture).

### Niche edits / link insertions

- **What it is:** A link added into an *existing* relevant article on another site.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Dofollow** typically (it sits in body content) - which is exactly why paid insertions are a violation unless qualified.
- **Typical value:** High when editorially justified (your resource genuinely improves an existing article). The link inherits the page's existing relevance and standing.
- **Risk:** Low if editorial and free; high if paid-for-followed (bought-followed-link violation). Many sellers offer paid insertions - that is the trap.
- **Language availability:** EN and FR - both viable through outreach.

### Resource pages

- **What it is:** A curated "useful links/tools/resources" page that lists your property.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Usually dofollow** (curated editorial lists).
- **Typical value:** Good - relevant, editorial, traffic-sending when you genuinely belong on the list.
- **Risk:** Low when you're a genuine fit; spraying unrelated resource pages degrades to low-quality directory behavior.
- **Language availability:** EN and FR - both common.

### Digital PR (HARO / journalist outreach)

- **What it is:** A citation/link earned by providing a journalist with an expert quote, data, or commentary.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Usually dofollow**, on genuinely authoritative news/industry sites.
- **Typical value:** Highest-tier - a real editor at a real publication chose to cite you. Strong ranking value plus brand authority. Best paired with an original asset (study/statistic) hosted on your own domain.
- **Risk:** Very low (these are the most defensible links there are).
- **Language availability:** EN - mature ecosystem (HARO/Connectively-style services). FR - fewer formal services, but direct journalist outreach and FR data-led PR work well.

### Q&A sites (Reddit, Quora, Stack Exchange)

- **What it is:** Answers/threads on Q&A and discussion platforms that link your resource.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Nofollow - without exception on the big three.** Reddit, Quora, and Stack Exchange all apply nofollow/UGC to outbound user links. **Correct the false claim head-on:** sources that tag Reddit or Quora "DOFOLLOW" are copy-paste errors and discredit the rest of their data.
- **Typical value:** Excellent **traffic and brand**, zero ranking credit. A genuinely helpful Reddit/Quora answer or a Stack Exchange post on a relevant topic can drive substantial qualified traffic and discovery for years.
- **Risk:** Low if genuinely helpful; high if self-promotional (these communities punish link-dropping fast, and Stack Exchange/Reddit remove spam aggressively).
- **Language availability:** EN - dominant. FR - Reddit/Quora have FR communities but thinner; Stack Exchange is largely EN.

### Brand mentions (linked and unlinked)

- **What it is:** Someone names your brand/product. Linked mentions are organic editorial links; *unlinked* mentions are reclaim opportunities (you ask for the link).
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Usually dofollow** when reclaimed on an editorial page.
- **Typical value:** High and very low-risk - the mention already exists, so the endorsement is genuine; you're just making it crawlable. Monitor with alerts (e.g., Google Alerts) to find them.
- **Risk:** Very low.
- **Language availability:** EN and FR - both, scaling with the property's existing visibility.

### Web 2.0 properties

- **What it is:** Content you publish on a free hosted platform (Medium, dev.to, etc.) linking to your property.
- **Typical dofollow status:** **Mostly nofollow** on the major platforms (outbound links are nofollowed).
- **Typical value:** Real, when treated as a genuine publishing channel with an audience (traffic/brand). Worthless-to-negative when used as a thin self-hosted link silo (PBN-lite pattern).
- **Risk:** Low as genuine publishing; medium-high as a link silo.
- **Language availability:** EN - abundant (dev.to, Medium). FR - Medium FR and FR dev/business platforms exist.

## Quick reference - honest defaults

| Source type | Typical dofollow | Primary value | Default risk | EN / FR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Forums | Nofollow/UGC | Traffic + brand | Low→high (scale) | EN rich / FR moderate |
| Profiles | Mostly nofollow | Brand presence | Low→high (mass) | EN rich / FR via global |
| Directories | Mixed | Discovery + minor | Low→high (mass) | EN rich / FR solid |
| Guest posts | Often dofollow | **Ranking** + traffic | Low→high (paid/optimized) | EN rich / FR rich |
| Niche edits | Usually dofollow | **Ranking** + traffic | Low→high (paid) | EN / FR both |
| Resource pages | Usually dofollow | **Ranking** + traffic | Low | EN / FR both |
| Digital PR | Usually dofollow | **Ranking** + authority | Very low | EN mature / FR direct |
| Q&A (Reddit/Quora/SE) | **Nofollow** | Traffic + brand | Low→high (self-promo) | EN dominant / FR thin |
| Brand mentions | Usually dofollow | **Ranking** + brand | Very low | EN / FR both |
| Web 2.0 | Mostly nofollow | Traffic + brand | Low→high (silo) | EN rich / FR exists |

Read the bolded **ranking** rows as the tactics worth real campaign effort; everything else is honest traffic/brand foundation. Match source type to property posture via `property-profiles.md`, verify each candidate via `qualification.md`, and never trust a list's dofollow claim without re-checking the live page (programmatically via managed APIs / remote scraping - never local scraping).
